Japan

╰┈➤ Japan is located in East Asia and is made up of islands in the Pacific Ocean. It lies to the east of China, Korea, and Russia, separated by the Sea of Japan.
╰┈➤ It's an archipelago of thousands of islands and is very mountainous, with forests covering over 70% of the land.
╰┈➤ Japan's population is 124 million and their capital is Tokyo.

╰┈➤ Samurai were Japan's aristocratic, highly disciplined warrior class, serving daimyos (feudal lords) from the 10th century until the 19th century. They were famous for their martial skills, armor, and swords (especially katanas).
╰┈➤ Samurai and Shoguns were in the warrior class and established feudal systems.
╰┈➤ There was a strict ethical code called Bushido (the way of the warrior) that emphasized loyalty, honor, and self-discipline.
╰┈➤ Japan was influenced by the Chinese and Korean. Early adoption of writing, Buddhism, government structures, and art where from them.
╰┈➤ The Meiji Restoration (1868) overthrew the feudal Tokugawa shogunate and restored power to the emperor, leading to modernization and westernization. It was important because it transformed Japan from an isolated, feudal state into a modern industrial and military power, allowing it to compete with Western nations and avoid colonization.

╰┈➤ This is Japan's flag.
╰┈➤ The red circle in the middle represents the sun, a central element in Japanese mythology and the Emperor's ancestry, signifying vitality, strength, and bravery.
╰┈➤ The white background symbolizes purity, honesty, integrity, and peace.
╰┈➤ It is powerful national symbol representing the "Land of the Rising Sun," serving as a unifying emblem of national identity, resilience, tradition, and cultural pride.

╰┈➤ Japan has 792 cities, with the largest city by population being Tokyo (capital), exceeding 37 million people.
╰┈➤ Tokyo is known for its modern skyscrapers, neon lights, advanced technology, Shibuya Crossing, etc.
╰┈➤ The second biggest city in Japan by population is Yokohama, a major port city located south of Tokyo, with a population of around 3.7 million.
╰┈➤ Yokohama is famous for its modern waterfront attractions, like the Landmark Tower and Minato Mirai 21. It served as a gateway for Western trade and cultural spots, such as Japan's largest Chinatown and the Cup Noodles Museum.
╰┈➤ A popular city many tourists explore include Kyoto. They are known for Buddhist temples, Shinto shrines, bamboo forests, and matcha, especially in the Uji region, where Japanese drink 1-3 cups for energy and health or more during ceremonial servings.
╰┈➤ Another popular city is Osaka, which is famous for its incredible food cuisine. Not only are they known as the "Kitchen of Japan," but they also have a lot of historical landmarks and modern attractions. Universal Studios Japan and the Osaka Aquarium are super popular among tourists and Osaka Castle is a historic castle with beautiful grounds.
